# Reader-down overlay banner over DiDi + alert vibration

The B-075 stopped-notification fires at the moment the projection dies — which is almost always at
screen lock, when nobody is looking. The moment that matters is the NEXT time the driver is in
DiDi/inDrive with the reader dead, silently missing verdicts. The accessibility service (overlay
host) keeps running when the projection dies, so it can show the alert in context.

## What to do
1. Overlay banner ("Lector apagado — toca para reiniciar") drawn by the accessibility service
   whenever the foreground app is an OCR-owned package AND the screen reader is not running
2. Tap → launch the consent flow (ACTION_START_SCREEN_READER); banner hides the moment the reader
   is running again or the driver leaves the app
3. Foreground detection: armed instantly by accessibility events, maintained by a light active
   -window poll that runs ONLY while the reader is down
4. Stopped-notification channel gains vibration (new channel id — channel settings are immutable
   once created)

## Acceptance criteria
- Reader dead + DiDi foreground → banner visible within ~3 s; tap opens consent over DiDi
- Banner gone the moment capture starts (no overlap with the verdict chip) or DiDi leaves
  the foreground
- No polling while the reader is running
- Banner never covers the bottom Aceptar zone (top-center placement)

## Result
Shipped 2026-06-12 (PR #7, merged 8134704). The accessibility service draws a tap-to-restart pill
over DiDi/inDrive whenever the OCR reader is dead AND the driver is in the host app — the silent-
miss window the B-075 stopped-notification (which fires at screen lock) can't cover. Event-armed
rise edge, polled-only-while-visible fall edge, gated on hasRunThisSession (recovery affordance,
never an onboarding nag), instant clear on switching to a non-OCR app. Stopped-notification channel
gained vibration (VIBRATE permission, _v2 channel). Hardened by a 12-agent adversarial workflow
(2 confirmed criticals fixed: always-on poll → poll-to-clear; never-enabled nag → hasRunThisSession)
and the Codex /ship battery (Security/Architecture/Correctness all PASS; 2 advisories fixed).
Deferred swipe-to-dismiss recorded as TD-027.

# OCR trip markers miscalibrated for DiDi

**Found on-device 2026-06-15** by accepting (then canceling) a real DiDi trip. The auto-ledger did
**not** record the accepted ride: the offer was never marked `ACCEPTED`, no economics trip opened, and
the only trips written were two **bare zero-value rows** (`trips` id=75/76, `offerId=NULL`, gross/dist/
dur all 0, `startedAt==endedAt`). Root cause: `OcrTripMarkers.DEFAULT_TRIP_MARKERS` matches the wrong
screens.

## What the OCR actually saw (two on-device accept→cancel runs)

**Run 2 — the decisive one (14:38, offer #16 DiDi $59.64, ACCEPTED by the driver).** The DB proves the
failure: offer #16 → `accepted=0, outcome=EXPIRED`, and **no trip row** was created. The active
**to-pickup navigation screen** (`Viaje aceptado`) was on screen ~6s and reads (rider address/rating
redacted — PII): `Viaje aceptado | Llegué por el pasajero | A ¡Vamos! | [PICKUP_ADDRESS] | [★ rating]
[N] viajes | 6 min 1.9 km | Llega antes de la(s) [HH:MM] | La grabación iniciará al empezar el viaje |
km/h | $7.02/km | $133/h`. → matches **ZERO** of the 13 markers.
("Viaje aceptado" ≠ "Viaje en curso"; "Llegué por el pasajero" ≠ "Llegar a"/"Recoger a"; no "Navegar"/
"En camino".) So the active-trip screen — not just the summary — is invisible to the classifier.

**Run 1 (14:07, accepted then canceled in ~4s).**
- Trip **details/summary** (rider name/addresses redacted — PII): `Detalles | Express | [PASSENGER] |
  [★ rating] | [PICKUP] → [DEST] | Cancelar | Soporte DiDi | Dejar de aceptar viajes` → matches
  **ZERO** markers → IDLE.
- **Cancel-reason dialog**: `…cancelaste el viaje | Punto de encuentro muy lejano | … | Tenía otro
  viaje en curso` → matches **"Viaje en curso"** + **"Punto de encuentro"** → TRIP → opens a bare trip
  (id=75), zeroed as noise. The markers fire on the *cancellation*, not the trip.

Net: the markers match **none** of DiDi's real trip screens (offer-accepted, to-pickup, details) and
**falsely** match the cancellation dialog. Exactly backwards. Both runs confirmed by string match + DB.

## Concrete anchors to add (from the real captures)
DiDi to-pickup / active-trip anchors (any one ⇒ trip): **`Viaje aceptado`**, **`Llegué por el
pasajero`**, **`La grabación iniciará al empezar el viaje`**, **`¡Vamos!`**. Exclude (noneOf, so a
cancellation never opens a trip): **`cancelaste el viaje`**, **`Por favor dinos por qué`**,
**`Punto de encuentro muy lejano`**. Still uncaptured: the on-trip (post-pickup, after "Llegué")
and trip-completion screens — grab those before finalizing the list.

## Caveat
Both runs were canceled before pickup, so a *completed* trip ≥ `minTripDurationMs` is still unvalidated
end-to-end — but the accept-detection failure is now proven independent of duration (offer #16 was
accepted and still produced `EXPIRED` + no trip).

## What to do
1. Capture real DiDi (and Uber) trip screens on-device: accept → drive to pickup → on-trip → complete.
   Record the OCR text of each phase (the app already dumps frames in debug).
2. Recalibrate `OcrTripMarkers`: add anchors that actually appear on DiDi trip screens (e.g. the
   passenger row + pickup/dest addresses + "Recoger"/"Iniciar viaje"/"Llegué"/"Soporte"), and
   **exclude the cancel-reason dialog** (a `noneOf`/guard: "cancelaste el viaje", "Por favor dinos por
   qué") so cancellations don't manufacture trips.
3. Consider splitting markers per-platform (the single shared list is a documented best-guess; see the
   `OcrTripMarkers` KDoc "NEEDS ON-DEVICE CALIBRATION").
4. Re-test the full lifecycle: accept → trip opens linked to the offer with the offer's economics →
   complete → trip closes with `endedAt`, drives a rollup.

## Acceptance criteria
- Accepting a DiDi offer and completing a trip ≥ min duration produces one `trips` row linked to the
  offer (`offerId` set), with the offer marked `ACCEPTED`, and closes on completion.
- A canceled offer/trip does NOT create a trip row (no cancel-dialog false-positive).
